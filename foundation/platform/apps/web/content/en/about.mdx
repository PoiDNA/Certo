## Mission

Certo Governance Institute builds a culture of good governance through independent assessment and promotion of governance standards in Poland and Europe. We operate at the intersection of law, technology and institutional ethics — where decisions made by governing bodies have real impact on citizens, employees and beneficiaries.

The Foundation's mission is to create a universally recognized standard for assessing the quality of governance, which will serve as a benchmark for public institutions, corporations, medical entities, defense organizations and the non-governmental sector.

## Independence

The Foundation is independent from the assessed entities, public authorities and commercial interests. This independence is guaranteed by:

- **Organizational structure** — The Foundation operates as a non-profit entity with no capital ties to the technical operator Certo ID PSA
- **Financing** — revenue from rating fees goes to a separate methodological fund
- **Procedures** — each rating undergoes a multi-stage verification process with mandatory involvement of external reviewers
- **Transparency** — the rating methodology is publicly available and subject to periodic updates in accordance with EU guidelines

## Two entities of the ecosystem

The Certo ecosystem consists of two separate legal entities:

**Certo Governance Institute** — an independent rating body based in Poland, owner of the Certo methodology, issues ratings and is responsible for their credibility.

**Certo ID PSA** — a commercial technical operator, maintains the IT infrastructure of the rating system, provides digital tools for assessed entities and their delegates.

This separation is a necessary condition for maintaining the independence of ratings — analogous to the separation of rating agencies from issuers of financial instruments.

## Assessment sectors

Certo assesses institutions in five sectors:

1. **Public sector** — local government units, offices, state administration
2. **Corporate sector** — commercial law companies, management boards, supervisory boards
3. **Medical sector** — hospitals, healthcare facilities, medical entities
4. **Defense sector** — defense industry entities, security system institutions
5. **Non-governmental sector** — non-governmental organizations, associations, foundations

## Compliance with European standards

The Certo methodology is based on rigorous European and international standards:

- European Commission guidelines on Corporate Governance
- ISO 37000 standards (Governance of Organizations)
- EU Directive on non-financial reporting (CSRD)
- OECD recommendations on corporate supervision
