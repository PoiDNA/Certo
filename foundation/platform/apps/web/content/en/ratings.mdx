## How to read a Certo Rating

Each Certo Rating consists of three elements:

**Certo Score** — synthetic assessment of governance quality on a scale from 0 to 100. A result above 70 indicates compliance with minimum governance standards required by the Institute.

**Certo Vector** — direction of change in governance quality since the last assessment:
- `++` — significant improvement in governance quality
- `+` — improvement
- `→` — no material change
- `−` — deterioration
- `−−` — significant deterioration requiring action

**Validity date** — A Certo Rating is issued for a period of 12 months. After this period expires, the entity must undergo reassessment to maintain active status.

## Rating verification

Each active rating is publicly verifiable. To verify a rating:

1. Find the entity in the database below
2. Click the Trust Badge Certo next to the entity's name
3. The system will display cryptographic proof of the rating along with the issuance date and assessment scope

Verification requires no login or registration. It is available to anyone, at any time.

## Sectors and assessment criteria

### Public sector

Assessment covers: structure of governing bodies, transparency of the decision-making process, access to public information, management of public assets, stakeholder service.

### Corporate sector

Assessment covers: composition and independence of the supervisory board, executive compensation policy, conflict of interest management, relations with minority shareholders, ESG reporting.

### Healthcare sector

Assessment covers: structure of facility management, patient safety procedures, medical risk management, compliance with NFZ and Ministry of Health regulations.

### Defense sector

Assessment covers: information security procedures, supply chain management, compliance with public contractor requirements, internal audit.

### Non-profit sector

Assessment covers: financing transparency, structure of statutory bodies, fulfillment of organizational mission, donor relationship management.
